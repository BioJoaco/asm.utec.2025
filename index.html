Who We Are

The ASM UTEC
Student Chapter

The ASM UTEC Student Chapter is an officially chartered student organization of the American Society for Microbiology at the Universidad de Ingeniería y Tecnología (UTEC) in Lima, Peru. We are a community of early-career scientists committed to advancing the science of microbiology, fostering scientific communication, and building meaningful connections among students and the broader academic community.

Our chapter serves as a bridge between UTEC's emerging scientific talent and the global microbiology community, providing a space for curiosity, growth, leadership, and service. We believe that science is more powerful when it is shared, accessible, and community-driven.

Our Journey Through 2025

From the first event of the year to our most ambitious initiatives, here is how 2025 unfolded for the ASM UTEC Student Chapter.

February 15, 2025
Give Life to Your Own 3D Heart!

An in-person workshop at UTEC combining a talk on 3D printing in medicine with a hands-on session. Participants learned about realistic anatomical modeling, explored a heartbeat simulator, and designed their own personalized 3D heart. Organized in collaboration with IEEE EMBS UTEC.

20 attendees · Collaborative with IEEE EMBS

February 21, 2025
What Can Microbes Teach Us About Our Environment?

A virtual talk organized in direct collaboration with ASM, featuring Dr. Davida S. Smyth, Associate Professor of Biology at Texas A&M University – San Antonio. Topics included microbial communities in built environments, antimicrobial materials, and the design of healthy spaces.

15 attendees · Direct ASM collaboration · International speaker

March 26, 2025
Biotechnology Applied to Bioremediation

Lic. Deysi Rendón Godoy (UNMSM graduate) presented her research on enzymatic biotechnology for environmental remediation — covering how enzymes can degrade contaminants and restore damaged ecosystems. The session sparked active academic debate on sustainability and biotechnological application.

20 attendees · High-engagement Q&A session

April 26, 2025
NEXOBIO 2025 — Interuniversity Symposium on Innovation in Biological Sciences

The flagship event of Semester 1: a full-day in-person symposium organized by ASM UTEC in collaboration with 7 other student organizations (PROYECTO HELA, BIOMICAS, SBE UTEC, BIOMED, IEEE EMBS UTEC, GIIA, and NEUROUTEC). Featured keynote lectures, discussion panels, interactive stands, and tech exhibitions bringing together students, researchers, and biotech companies.

190 attendees · 7 partner orgs · Industry & academia · Plans for NEXOBIO 2026

An international virtual microcongress organized by ASM Peru together with student chapters from Colombia, Guatemala, Venezuela, and El Salvador. The event included keynote lectures, student project presentations, and scientific discussion forums — promoting regional exchange and microbiology engagement across Latin America.

Workshops and talks covered biotechnology, space, mathematics, artificial intelligence, and innovation — all with a hands-on and motivating approach to inspire the next generation of scientists.

Fermentation Workshop: The World of Kefir

A hands-on workshop led by Prof. Alejandra Ratti and student Daniel Cabrejos at UTEC. Intelligent Packaging with Anthocyanins

Semester 2 opened with a talk on smart anthocyanin-based packaging systems that monitor food freshness and extend shelf life — offering sustainable alternatives for the food industry. A strong start connecting microbiology with applied environmental science and food safety.

Agar Art Workshop — Microbes Make the World Go Round

Aligned with the global ASM initiative "Microbes Make The World Go Round," participants used pigmented microorganisms as brushes and agar plates as canvases to create biological artworks. The event was held at UPC Villa, expanding the chapter's reach beyond UTEC's campus for the first time.

November 3–7, 2025
ASM CULTURE Week — 5-Day Science & Microbiology Festival

The most ambitious initiative of the year: a 5-day scientific festival with 14 individual activities spanning keynote talks, international virtual conferences, hands-on workshops, a Journal Club, an Agar Art Competition, and bioinformatics training. Topics included antimicrobial resistance, genomics, biosensors, synthetic biology, and AI-driven antibiotic discovery. International speakers joined from India, Chile, Brazil, and Mexico.

Throughout 2025, the ASM UTEC Student Chapter organized targeted activities designed to accelerate the professional growth of our members and the broader student community.

We believe that scientific excellence goes hand in hand with professional readiness — and we worked actively to create the conditions for both.

🧠

Scientific Workshops & Seminars

5 hands-on workshops were delivered across the year, covering fermentation and kefir production, agar art and microbial creativity, food safety and microbial isolation, MBL detection using EDTA inhibition, and Benchling for synthetic plasmid design. Each session was designed for active participation, not passive attendance.

🧭

Expert Talks & Mentorship

Over 24 invited speakers shared expertise with our community — including researchers from UNMSM, UPCH, ESSALUD, Texas A&M, USP Brazil, Universidad de Talca, and Armed Forces Medical College India. Students gained direct exposure to active scientific careers across academia, clinical research, and industry.

🌐

Networking Opportunities

Through NEXOBIO 2025, the Microcongreso LATAM, and ASM CULTURE Week, students connected with peers, researchers, and professionals across Peru and 4 other Latin American countries. NEXOBIO alone facilitated partnerships with biotech companies and research laboratories.

📝

Scientific Communication Training

The chapter's Journal Club (held during ASM CULTURE Week) gave members practice in reading, analyzing, and presenting scientific papers to their peers — a foundational skill for early-career researchers. Activities like the Agar Art Competition also encouraged creative, public-facing science communication.

🔗

Connections to the ASM Ecosystem

We organized a direct collaboration with ASM's global team for the February virtual talk with Dr. Davida S. Smyth (Texas A&M). We also coordinated the ASM LATAM Microcongress with 4 other national ASM chapters, reinforcing student awareness of and participation in the broader ASM network.

🏆

Recognition & Achievement

The chapter was awarded the UTEC HONORS Prize for Best University Community in 2025 — the highest institutional recognition for student organizations at UTEC. This award validates the consistency, quality, and impact of our work throughout the year.

⭐ Award Criterion 2

Community Building,
Engagement & Outreach

Science is most powerful when it reaches beyond the walls of the laboratory. In 2025, the ASM UTEC Student Chapter engaged with communities inside and outside the university — driven by a belief that microbiology is relevant, exciting, and accessible to everyone.

Our outreach efforts prioritized inclusion, curiosity, and the idea that scientific culture is something to be built collectively — not reserved for a privileged few.

🌱

"Science communication is an act of generosity — it means believing that everyone deserves access to knowledge."

[Insert quote author or keep as general statement]

📢

Science Communication

From agar art workshops to food safety demonstrations with live bacterial cultures on agar plates, we consistently made microbiology visible, tangible, and relatable. Our activities were designed to shift perception — from microbiology as a niche discipline to one that shapes food, health, and the environment around us.

🏫

K-12 Educational Outreach

Through SUMVISSION 2025 — part of the SumakSTEM movement — we brought science directly to school-age students, covering biotechnology, artificial intelligence, mathematics, and space exploration in an interactive format. Reaching young students before they enter university is one of our most meaningful long-term investments.

🧫

Microbiology Awareness Campaigns

The Agar Art Workshop at UPC Villa was directly aligned with ASM's global initiative "Microbes Make The World Go Round," raising awareness of microbial science in a creative and accessible way. Our ASM CULTURE Week also raised public awareness around antimicrobial resistance — one of the most pressing global health challenges.

♿

Inclusion & Accessibility

The vast majority of our events were offered completely free of charge. Workshops like the Kefir Fermentation session and the Agar Art activities were explicitly designed for students with no prior microbiology background, ensuring everyone could participate, learn, and feel part of the scientific community.

🤲

Multi-Institution Campus Community

NEXOBIO 2025 united 8 student organizations from across UTEC's student ecosystem. Beyond our own campus, we held events at UPC Villa and engaged students through virtual platforms — building a community that extends well beyond a single institution.

🤝

Integration & Team Culture

Beyond public-facing events, we held two internal integration sessions throughout the year — dedicated spaces for members to connect, discuss the chapter's direction, and build the kind of trust and camaraderie that sustains a community over time. Community starts from within.

Our chapter is proud to be part of the global ASM community. In 2025, we worked actively to represent ASM's mission and values within UTEC, in Lima, and across Peru — ensuring that the microbiology society's reach extends meaningfully into the Latin American academic landscape.

We see ourselves not just as a student club, but as ambassadors of microbiology and of ASM's vision for a world where microbial sciences contribute to a healthier, more sustainable planet.

🌎

"Being part of ASM means being part of something much larger than ourselves — a global community united by curiosity and scientific rigor."

Promoting ASM's Mission

Every event we organized — from biosensor talks to fermentation workshops — was rooted in ASM's core mission: advancing microbial sciences for the benefit of humanity. The ASM brand and values were present in our materials, communications, and the way we framed science to our audiences.

🔖

ASM Membership & Visibility

We actively promoted ASM membership and resources throughout the year, highlighting journals, career tools, and the global ASM network to our members and collaborating institutions. The direct ASM collaboration in February (Dr. Davida Smyth talk) was an especially visible demonstration of ASM's reach and value.

Engagement with ASM Global Initiatives

Our October Agar Art Workshop was explicitly aligned with ASM's worldwide campaign "Microbes Make The World Go Round." By connecting our local activities to ASM's global agenda, we helped make that agenda visible and meaningful to students in Lima.

Building the Latin American ASM Network

Reporting & Accountability

The chapter maintained detailed activity records for both academic semesters of 2025, including attendance data, speaker profiles, event objectives, and outcome assessments. Transparency and documentation are core to how we represent ASM and demonstrate our contribution to the microbiology community.

Institutional Recognition of ASM's Impact

Winning the UTEC HONORS Award for Best University Community is, in part, a recognition of what ASM stands for: scientific rigor, community engagement, and student leadership. Our chapter's success reflects positively on the ASM name within Peru's leading engineering and technology university.

Our Most Impactful Work

Symposium · Community Building · April 26

NEXOBIO 2025 — Interuniversity Symposium on Innovation in Biological Sciences

The first edition of NEXOBIO was a milestone for student-led science in Peru. Organized entirely by ASM UTEC in coalition with 7 student organizations across different disciplines, this full-day event at UTEC's auditorium brought together students, researchers, and biotech industry representatives for keynote talks, discussion panels, interactive stands, and technology exhibitions.

Why it mattered: It demonstrated that students can lead large-scale, multi-institutional scientific events — and that microbiology naturally sits at the intersection of health, technology, and society.

Outcomes 190 attendees · 7 co-organizing student groups (PROYECTO HELA, BIOMICAS, SBE UTEC, BIOMED, IEEE EMBS UTEC, GIIA, NEUROUTEC) · Partnerships with industry and research laboratories established · Unanimously proposed for replication in 2026.

Multi-day Festival · Professional Development · November 3–7

ASM CULTURE Week — 5-Day Microbiology Science Festival

ASM CULTURE was the most comprehensive initiative in the chapter's history: a 5-day scientific festival with 14 individual activities spanning talks, international virtual conferences, hands-on workshops, a Biosafety Workshop, a Journal Club, an Agar Art Competition, and bioinformatics training. Topics included antimicrobial resistance, genomics and public health, AI-driven antibiotic discovery, synthetic biology, and biosensor design. International speakers joined from India, Chile, Brazil, and Mexico.

Why it mattered: It consolidated ASM UTEC as the leading microbiology student community in Lima, attracting faculty, industry partners, and students from other universities — in a single, cohesive week-long program.

Outcomes ~600 total engagements · 14 activities across 5 days · Speakers from 4+ countries · Featured in UTEC's Bioengineering department communications · Agar Art Competition held with institutional prize funding (S/ 87.50 budget).

International · Virtual · April 28 – May 4

Microcongreso Virtual ASM LATAM 2025

A landmark international virtual congress co-organized by ASM Peru with student chapters from Colombia, Guatemala, Venezuela, and El Salvador. The event offered keynote lectures, student-led research presentations, and collaborative scientific forums — creating a genuine space for early-career scientists across Latin America to connect, share knowledge, and strengthen ties within the ASM global community.

Why it mattered: It positioned ASM UTEC not just as a campus chapter, but as an active node in the Latin American microbiology network — directly advancing ASM's mission of global scientific collaboration.

Outcomes 120 attendees (20% above expected) · 5 countries represented · Highly rated in post-event surveys · Proposed and accepted as an annual ASM LATAM event going forward.

Reflection

"

2025 showed us that students, when given space and trust, can build something genuinely meaningful — for science, for their community, and for each other.

2025 was a year of 30 activities, 1,079+ attendees, and 24+ speakers — but more than the numbers, it was a year of consistent presence. The ASM UTEC Student Chapter showed up every month, for every kind of student: those curious about fermentation, those concerned about antimicrobial resistance, those who had never held a pipette but were fascinated by the idea of painting with bacteria.

We ran a 5-day scientific festival. We co-organized an international microcongress spanning 5 countries. We brought biotechnology to school-age students through SUMVISSION. We designed activities with biosafety requirements and lab coats, and others with nothing but curiosity as a prerequisite. This range — from rigorous to accessible, from local to international — is precisely what we believe a student chapter should be.

The UTEC HONORS Award for Best University Community confirmed what we had been building all year: a chapter that doesn't just organize events, but genuinely serves its community. We are proud of what 2025 became — and we are already working to make 2026 even more impactful.
